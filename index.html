  • Agriculture Crops Yield | R

    This project investigates the impact of rainfall and temperature on crop yield using R. By analyzing data on these environmental factors and their correlation with crop productivity, the study aims to identify optimal conditions for maximizing yields. The findings will provide actionable recommendations for farmers and suggest strategies for mitigating the effects of extreme weather conditions, contributing to more sustainable agricultural practices.

    • What is the effect of Rainfall on Crop Yield?

    2- Gather Information
    • Amount of Rainfall.
    • Temperature.
    • Total crops yield.
    3- Potential Solutions
    • Higher rainfall and moderate temperatures lead to higher crop yields.
    • There are optimal ranges of rainfall and temperature beyond which crop yield does not increase significantly or may decrease.
    4- Evaluate alternatives
    • Data Analysis:
      • Perform a multiple regression analysis to determine the combined effect of rainfall and temperature on crop yield.
      • Use scatter plots to visualize the relationships for different crops.
      • Calculate correlation coefficients to quantify the strength of the relationships.
    • Visualization:
      • Create scatter plots of Rainfall_mm vs. Yield_tons_per_hectare and Temperature_Celsius vs. Yield_tons_per_hectare for each crop.
      • Use plots to visualize the combined effect of rainfall and temperature on yield.
    5- Best Solution Identify the optimal ranges of rainfall and temperature for each crop.
    6- Implementation
    • Recommendations:
      • Provide guidelines for farmers on the optimal ranges of rainfall and temperature for different crops.
      • Suggest irrigation and temperature management practices to maintain optimal conditions.
